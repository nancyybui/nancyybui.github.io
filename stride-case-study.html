Designing a Career Fair

I spearhead branding, creative direction, illustration, and web design for UC San Diego's first design-centric career fair in addition to creating strategic partnerships and leading recruiting efforts.

Overview

Design at UCSD launched a new initiative this summer to hold UC San Diego's first design-centric career fair. To kickoff, I designed a new brand identity to capture the spirit of the event, while keeping in mind the core values and brand identity of Design at UCSD. Materials include a promotional website, tabloids and flyers, marketing collateral, event materials, sticker sheets, t-shirts, and more.

Designing the Brand

Right off the bat, I chatted with the members of Design at UCSD to get a better feel for what the event was trying to accomplish, and what the brand experience would be like.

It was decided that the brand identity should convey the friendliness and talent of our design community to employers, give a sense of approachability to encourage students to come out, and to showcase the diversity that is present in our design community at Design at UCSD.

We decided on a human-heavy brand, with characters striding towards success through design careers. Small motifs such as sticky notes and pencils are used to visualize design culture.

Website

We strayed away from the traditional one-page event websites in order to showcase the intent of the event. Since it was the first time we were putting on the event, it was crucial that our event's unique distinctions as a design-centric career fair was clear right off the bat.

I designed the web pages and then worked with our web developer to work on micro-interactions, hover states, and animations, in order to ensure that everything was shipped correctly.

The Day Of

The event was a success! It was widely marketed, and we had over 300 young designers come out to showcase their work, in addition to the twelve companies in attendance. Thank you to everyone who came out.
