Design at UCSD

Growing a Design Community

Overview
Design at UCSD is a pre-professional organization that aims to be at the forefront of design thinking at UC San Diego. Through hosting events, talks, and interactive workshops, our hope is to bridge the gap between design academia and industry while equipping members with the skills and knowledge needed to succeed in the rapidly evolving and innovative field of human-centered design.

Color + Typography
Last spring, I planned and led a workshop focused around color and typography. My co-chair and I taught fundamental visual, brand, and graphic design principles that students can use in their interaction design projects. We created both the workshop content in addition to the interactive activities to help students take the skills they learned and actively put them into practice with our guidance and mentorship.

Designing web pages
I designed the website for Design Frontiers, a day-long design sprint aimed at bridging academia and industry through providing students with the opportunity to tackle real-world design challenges.
The one-page website was designed for easy skimming, providing a quick and intuitive user experience. Content like an overview, schedule, FAQs, and event summaries were included in order to provide the audience with an appropriate amount of information and context prior to the event.
I worked with our web developer to build the site and ensure that the site was adaptable to allow information to easily be inputted and changed as we neared the event.
