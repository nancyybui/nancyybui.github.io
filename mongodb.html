MongoDB

Designing for databases

Overview
This past summer, I was given the incredible opportunity to intern for MongoDB’s Brand Experience Team. Over a course of  ten weeks, I learned the importance of building a successful brand that translates across company culture and the customer experience. My work has continued throughout the year as I am currently working on their team as a Brand Design Contractor. Fun fact: I was the first MongoDB intern based in Palo Alto!
Role
I drive company spirit across global teams by producing a variety of visual brand assets, creating strategic iconography. Spearheaded an effort to simplify the designer-to-developer workflow by identifying pain points in brand collateral production.

Project Synthesis
I identified pain points in the designer-to-developer workflow after analyzing the current process. I researched different methods to address the core issues and proposed a brand new workflow to the team. I wrote a case study on this project to promote my experience on MongoDB’s Brand Design team.

Marketing collateral creation
My responsibilities have also included creating brand and marketing collateral for various events and initiative, both internal and external. The work included tabloids, social media banners, promotional materials, internal branding, and more. I worked under the creative director and various members of the brand design team.

[3] Designing for initiatives

[Fig 3] Social media digital kit for MongoDB 4.0
Designing social media kits and web pages
MongoDB 4.0 was launched shortly before I had started. As a result, all the outflowing materials followed the branding for the initiative. The branding affected social media kits, web pages, and other pieces of collateral.
An aspect of my work involved creating multiple digital kits for MongoDB marketing. Given that these were intended to be used for marketing purposes, I ensured that they were built in a way that allowed for accessibility and customization. I presented these kits to the Creative Director before handing them off to each respective team.
I also worked with the team to build various webpages. I designed these pages to emulate MongoDB’s visual style and worked with a front-end engineer to implement each one.
